Gardening

Daffodils, Tulips, Hyacinths, and Grape Muscari are some of the bulbs I tried out this year. These spring-blooming bulbs should be planted in the fall a few weeks before the first hard frost. They bring in some lovely color in time for spring to begin.

Tete-a-tete daffodil - zone 4 through 8 bulb - is a very early blooming miniature daffodil. They only get up to about 6-8" tall, but they can have multiple flowers per stem. Daffodils are wonderful perennials, so leave them be until the foliage dies back to ensure a strong bloom for the next year.

Juniper Procumbens - zone 4 through 9 conifer - is a popular shrub among landscapers and bonsai enthusiasts. They are easy to grow, requiring little effort, and they make an excellent first-time bonsai plant. Find one at your local nursery and try it out!

Proven Winner's Bloom-a-thon Pink Double Reblooming Azalea is absolutely beautiful with it's many ruffled flowers. This Rhododendron blooms in the spring for about 4-6 weeks, then again mid summer for another long show!
